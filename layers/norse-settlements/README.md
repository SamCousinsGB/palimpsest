# Norse settlements (NW England)

Place-name evidence for Scandinavian settlement around the Mersey and Ribble,
centred on the Hiberno-Norse colony traditionally dated to **c.902 AD**, when
Ingimund and his followers — Norse settlers expelled from Dublin — were granted
land on Wirral by Æthelflæd, Lady of the Mercians.

## What the map shows

53 settlements whose names contain Old Norse (or Norse-transmitted Gaelic)
elements, coloured by element type:

| Group | Element | Reading |
|---|---|---|
| þing | *þing-vǫllr* | assembly field — Thingwall occurs **twice**, once each side of the Mersey, implying organised, self-governing Norse communities |
| -bý | *býr* | farm/settlement — the classic Scandinavian settlement suffix; densest cluster on Wirral |
| kirkja | *kirkja* | church — Christian (largely Hiberno-Norse) communities |
| stǫð | *stǫð* | landing place — Toxteth and Croxteth, Liverpool's two named Norse beaching points |
| erg | *ærgi/erg* | shieling (summer pasture) — a Gaelic loan-word, fingerprinting settlers who came via Ireland/the Isles |
| Gaelic | — | purely Irish names (Noctorum, Liscard) given by Irish speakers within the Norse community |
| landscape | *melr, dalr, lundr, brekka, nes, kjarr…* | topographic names |
| hybrid | ON + OE *tūn* | Norse landowners in English naming patterns — language contact, not colonisation |

Fill strength encodes scholarly certainty (solid = consensus, faint = probable,
hollow = disputed).

## Timeline

The `attested` property is the year of the **first surviving written record**
(Domesday Book = 1086 for most). It is *not* the settlement date — nearly all
of these places were founded in the 10th century. Documentation, not
occupation. Scroll the timeline to watch the record accumulate from Domesday
through the 13th-century charters.

## Caveats

- Coordinates are modern settlement centroids (geocoded via Nominatim), not
  excavated sites. Argarmeols is placed approximately — the village was lost
  to the sea by the 14th century.
- Etymologies follow mainstream scholarship but place-name studies argue;
  the `certainty` field flags the contested ones.
- Attestation years for non-Domesday names are approximate ("c." dates).
